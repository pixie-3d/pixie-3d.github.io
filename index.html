Why learn physics fields?

Photorealistic 3D reconstructions (NeRF, Gaussian Splatting) capture geometry & appearance but lack physics. Existing test‑time optimisation methods are slow and scene‑specific. Pixie trains a lightweight 3D U‑Net that maps CLIP‑distilled feature grids to dense material fields (material ID, Young’s modulus, Poisson’s ratio, density) in a single forward pass, enabling real‑time Material Point Method simulations.

PixieVerse Dataset

  • 1 624 high‑quality Objaverse assets
  • 10 semantic super‑classes
  • 6 material constitutive models
  • Per‑voxel annotations of E, ν, ρ & material ID
  • Generated via Gemini‑2.5‑Pro + physics heuristics

Method Overview

Multi‑view RGB images are encoded by a CLIP‑NeRF yielding a 3D feature grid. A compact 3D U‑Net predicts material fields that are transferred onto Gaussian splats and simulated with an MPM solver at real‑time rates.

Results

On the PixieVerse benchmark, Pixie outperforms DreamPhysics, OmniPhysGS and NeRF2Physics by 2.2–4.6× in Gemini‑Pro realism while running 10³× faster.

Zero‑shot Transfer to Real Scenes

Without any real‑scene supervision, Pixie predicts plausible material fields for captured NeRFs and produces realistic wind animations aligned with human expectations.

Ablation Study

Replacing CLIP with raw RGB or occupancy features severely degrades material accuracy (−20‑40 %) and doubles continuous errors, confirming the importance of semantic priors.
